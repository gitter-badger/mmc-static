How can you take the next step to accelerate your career? Join us for an all-female event hosted at the Notre Dame Michigan Avenue Campus. Panelists will discuss their careers, the value of a graduate degree, and how they maintained balance – academic, professional and personal – throughout their Notre Dame experience.

Schedule of Events:
6:00 pm - 6:30 pm CT: Registration and Networking
6:30 pm - 7:30 pm CT: Speaker Barb Singer Cheng and Panel Discussion

Featured Panelists:

Julie Talbot is currently a Vice President, Branch Office Manager of Personal and Workplace Investing for Fidelity Investments. In her current role, Julie manages the Grand Rapids and South Bend Investor Centers and is responsible for creating and leading strategies to develop the markets in the communities the branches serve. Julie was a member of the South Bend Executive MBA Class of 2015. Julie, her husband Eugene, and their daughter Abigail reside in Lowell, Michigan.

Ami McReynolds is the Senior Vice President of Network Development with Feeding America, the nation's leading domestic hunger relief charity. In her role, she partners with leaders across the 200-member Feeding America network, building organizational capacity in pursuit of common goals and strategies, and strengthening the network through member engagement. Ami was a member of the Chicago Executive MBA Class of 2015.

Monica Luechtedfeld is a recognized leader in E Commerce and Internet Retailing and is the former Executive Vice President of Office Depot. As a senior executive, she held various leadership positions during her twenty-year career with Office Depot. In 2012, Monica founded her own consultancy firm to provide advisory services in E Commerce strategy as well as online marketing and digital retailing. Monica was a member of the Chicago Executive MBA Class of 2013.

Maggie Wartik is the General Manager of Chicago Tribune’s Young Adult vertical, overseeing the print, digital, and mobile operations for the #1 city brand, RedEye, as well as the digital entertainment hub Metromix, extensive blogger network ChicagoNow, and teen pub The Mash. Maggie is passionate about business transformation in an ever-changing media landscape. Maggie was a member of the Chicago Executive MBA Class of 2015.
