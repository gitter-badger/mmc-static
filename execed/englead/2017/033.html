April 18-20, 2017

BE A POWERFUL FORCE FOR GOOD IN THE WORLD.

The College of Engineering and Mendoza College of Business at the University of Notre Dame is proud of its mission and efforts to fulfill Fr. Sorin’s stirring words to be a powerful force for good in the world. We do this in many ways — through teaching, research, and community outreach. I am very excited to share the dates of one of our newest efforts in this regard designed to develop the leadership skills of our engineering alumni. The College of Engineering, in conjunction with the Stayer Center for Executive Education in the Mendoza College of Business, is again offering Engineering Leadership—becoming a more effective leader. It is designed to help engineers who are ready for, or already have undertaken, higher levels of responsibility. This is a critical transition for any engineering leader. We are pleased to offer assistance three times in 2017 to help make this transition successfully:

  • April 18-20, 2017 (Stayer Center for Executive Education at Notre Dame)
  • October 3-5, 2017 (Stayer Center for Executive Education at Notre Dame)
  • November 14-16, 2017 (Notre Dame's downtown Chicago location)

Notre Dame’s Engineering Leadership is designed to:

  • Provide participants with a proven and effective way to successfully make the transition from individual contributor to technical leader.
  • Develop technical professionals to be competent managers and leaders with effective skills and goal-oriented and impactful plans to lead individual and team efforts.
  • Develop awareness and perspective of the interpersonal and affective issues related to leadership, allowing engineers to continue to advance their influence and impact in their organizations.

This is consistent with the College’s mission to be, first and foremost, a powerful force for good in the world. If you aspire to be that force for good, I strongly encourage you to consider this program. It is a unique opportunity to return to campus and continue your learning and development.

I look forward to welcoming you back to campus for this exciting opportunity.

Yours in Notre Dame,

Peter Kilpatrick
McCloskey Dean of Engineering

P.S. This program is open to any interested individual, whether or not they graduated from The College of Engineering at the University of Notre Dame. Register yourself or support a key individual at your company to attend.
